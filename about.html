Hi!! I'm Roshini Saravanakumar, and I'm really excited that you're here. Let me tell you my story.

My journey began two years ago as an incoming freshman at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. I've lived a majority of my life in California and I rarely travel, so the fact that I was halfway across the country with no connections absolutely blew. my. mind. I had applied into Computer Engineering as a last-minute effort to choose between Computer Science and Electrical Engineering, so I was slightly nervous, especially since I barely knew how to code prior to college. Little did I know at the time, in less than a semester, I was certain that Computer Engineering was truly my passion and I would simply love every ECE course I took.

In the past two years, I have built an operating system from scratch, recreated one of my favorite childhood games for the FPGA, created visualizations and user interfaces to create learning experiences from huge datasets, and successfully completed so many projects that I had only ever dreamt of being a part of. Outside of school, I love exploring my creativity in its countless other forms. Prior to college, I had picked up bullet journaling as a method to track my tasks and achievements. However, through college, my bullet journal has become my go-to location to explore and record my thoughts, artwork, calligraphy, to-do lists and passions. Around the same time that I began bullet journaling, I became interested in brush calligraphy and I have been practicing it ever since. I have always had an inexplicable love for expressing my feelings and ideas and recreating my reality through art, especially through watercolor and abstract doodles. Many of these works find their home in my bullet journal, but a good portion of them find their way to my wall or to my friends and family.

I've also been a book nerd practically since the moment I laid finger on the countless folktale and fairytale books my mom read to me as a child. In the past few years, I have dipped my feet into the self-help genre, in an effort to discover habits and ideas that would help me piece my life back together after being in a mental slump for a year. Many of the books that I picked up, starting with The Book of Joy by the fourteenth Dalai Lama and Desmond Tutu in my freshman year of college, drastically shifted the lens through which I see our world. They have taught me skills from time management to focusing on the present moment, and most importantly, they have taught me that I am capable of learning and achieving so much if I simply go for it. I take this philosophy with me into my everyday interactions with others and the world, treating everyone with compassion and kindness, and exploring what it truly means to be alive and thriving.
